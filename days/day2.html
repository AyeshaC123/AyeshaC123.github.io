How can technology support LGBTQ topics for a school curricullum?

Where can it start?

Schools are starting to become more aware of ways to implement LGBTQ+ topics and education into the school curriculum to support their LGBTQ+ students. One of these methods include integrating technology as a tool for the students. This includes open educational resources which are materials for teaching or learning that are either in the public domain or have been released under a license that allows them to be freely changed and shared with others

Examples Include:

  • Documentaries or videos that depict LGBTQ+ individuals talking about real-time issues and experiences they face, where students can be introduced to different perspectives and awareness as well as formulate their own thoughts on the topic.
  • A digital lesson plan that contains digital activites to complete that can be bpth fun and interactive as well as informative on LGBTQ+ topics.
  • Online Games that allow students to learn the difference between sexual and gender identities and the types of gender identities.
  • Having students complete an online course or curriculum that teaches LGBTQ+ topics.
  • Digital Music that can be played from famous musicians that discuss LGBTQ+ issues.
